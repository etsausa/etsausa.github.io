HANGOUT BETA

During the summer of 2020, I was visiting a friend at the Rochester Institute of Technology. While my friend was grabbing us some burritos from the food court, I was taking a look at the various posters of the student organizations and noticed an ad from a group of developers who needed a UX/UI designer. Since the pandemic was ravenging my post-grad employment opportunities, I decided to give it a shot and sent the group an email.

This is how I joined the group at Amici.ai, which was called Hangout Beta when I joined. A group of RIT students set out to create a social media app that was all about getting people to make real life plans - an app to help people hangout.

The team was utilizing RIT’s accelerator program which helped alumni based start-ups secure funding for their projects. This was an invaluable asset that helped kickstart the idea from a side project to a proper start up. Our project manager, Aibek, was able to use the resources from the accelerator program to push our MVP to secure long term funding.

As UI/UX designer, I worked in figma to design some of the more intricate interactions that the app needed. For the first MVP, the app only contained a feed wherein users could post plans to. For the next MVP, the team wanted to develop a more robust tool kit to make hanging out easier. Another RIT alumni had initially worked as the designer on the project before me, so I had a set of components and a design language to work on. This was great as it enabled me to focus on designing interactions.

Two of the tools that I worked on were more robust chat features and a scheduler. I also refreshed the basic feed to a new design that incorporated these new features. In our weekly team meetings, we would discuss the features that our user research revealed. I would design lofi designs in photoshop, review with the team, and then move on to hifi designs in figma. Once the hifi’s were approved they moved on to dev.

Working at Hangout was a great experience. Getting to collaborate with students from RIT was really enriching for my personal development as a media professional, as I got to learn how different types of students work outside of my own college community. In addition, I learned how a start-up environment works and how a team can motivate themselves to create their own professional setting.
